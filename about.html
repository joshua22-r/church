Our History

St. Janani Church of Uganda Kabalagala was established in 1968 as a small worshiping community serving the growing Anglican population in the Kabalagala area. Named after Janani Luwum, the former Archbishop of Uganda who became a martyr for his faith, our church has stood as a beacon of hope and spiritual nourishment for over five decades.

From humble beginnings meeting in a temporary structure, the church has grown to become one of the prominent Anglican congregations in Kampala. Our current church building was consecrated in 1985 and has since undergone several expansions to accommodate our growing congregation.

Our Beliefs

As part of the Church of Uganda, a member of the Anglican Communion, we uphold the historic Christian faith as expressed in the Apostles' and Nicene Creeds. We are committed to biblical teaching, sacramental worship, and evangelical witness.

  • We believe in the Holy Trinity: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit
  • We affirm the authority of Scripture as God's revealed word
  • We celebrate the sacraments of Baptism and Holy Communion
  • We uphold the historic Anglican tradition

Church Milestones

Significant moments in our journey of faith

1968

Church Founding

St. Janani Church was established by a small group of Anglican believers meeting in a rented space in Kabalagala.

1975

First Permanent Structure

The congregation completed construction of its first permanent worship structure, accommodating 150 worshippers.

1985

Current Church Consecration

The current church building was completed and consecrated by the Bishop of Kampala Diocese.

1998

30th Anniversary

Celebrated our 30th anniversary with special services and community outreach programs.

2010

Major Renovation

Completed extensive renovations to expand seating capacity and modernize facilities.

2018

Golden Jubilee

Celebrated 50 years of ministry with year-long events and the launch of our community development fund.
